# Budget-Dashboard
Short Description / Purpose
An interactive Power BI dashboard designed to track personal revenue stream performance, break down multi-year spending habits, and monitor investment allocations. This tool provides a clear, consolidated view of financial health, enabling data-driven decisions to maximize savings and optimize long-term wealth building.
Tech Stack
1.Business Intelligence: Power BI Desktop
2.Data Modeling & Analytics: DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
3.Data Transformation: Power Query
4.Data Source: Excel / CSV structured financial logs
Data Source
The dashboard ingests historical personal finance records structured across chronological logs. The dataset contains itemized granular details including transaction dates, income origins (e.g., freelance platforms, side hustles), categorical expense tagging (e.g., fixed vs. variable costs), and investment channel breakdowns.
Features / Highlights
1. Business (Personal Finance) Problem
Managing multiple variable income streams and tracking diverse expenditure categories across several years often leads to financial fragmentation. Without a centralized view, it is difficult to accurately pinpoint where capital is leaking, whether saving targets are being met, or if investments are scaling proportionally with earnings.

2. Goal of the Dashboard
The primary goal is to aggregate multi-year financial data into a singular, scannable command center. It aims to provide real-time clarity on net cash flow (Balance), identify top expense drivers, monitor side-hustle revenue performance, and ensure consistent capital allocation toward wealth-building investments.

3. Walkthrough of Key Visuals
As displayed in Snapshot of the Dashboard.png, the report is structured into intuitive, highly scannable analytical zones:

High-Level Financial KPIs (Top Banner): Displays essential macroeconomic health metrics including total Balance ($535\text{K}$), Income ($977\text{K}$), Expenses ($77\text{K}$), Investments ($365\text{K}$), and total Outflows ($442\text{K}$) to establish an immediate financial baseline.

Granular Chronological Ledger (Left Table): A detailed tabular breakdown mapped by Year-Month that cross-references month-over-month fluctuations in Income, Expenses, and Investments.

Expenses by Category (Center Donut Chart): Visually segments variable and fixed costs. Rent represents the overwhelming majority at $58.54\%$, followed closely by Groceries, Home Appliances, and Food.

Income by Sub-Category (Bottom-Center Funnel): Explicitly isolates the performance of main revenue channels, tracking the healthy distribution between Youtube ($591\text{K}$) and Upwork ($386\text{K}$).

Investments by Category (Bottom-Right Bar Chart): Compares portfolio diversification, contrasting allocations into Metal vs. traditional Shares.

Monthly Averages & Global Slicers (Right Banner & Sidebar): Features standalone cards calculating rolling benchmarks (such as Average Monthly Expenses at $2.56\text{K}$) alongside a quick-toggle Year Filter ($2022\text{–}2024$) for seamless time-series deep dives.

4. Business Impact & Insights
Expense Optimization: Highlighting that Rent and Groceries swallow the largest shares allows for more realistic lifestyle inflation adjustments and fixed-cost budgeting.
Revenue Performance Tracking: Funnel tracking reveals that Youtube generates over $60\%$ of total revenue, indicating where personal time and marketing effort yield the highest return on investment (ROI).
Wealth Accumulation Efficiency: By maintaining an Average Monthly Income of $32.58\text{K}$ against an Average Monthly Expense of $2.56\text{K}$, the dashboard proves an incredibly high savings-to-income ratio, validating aggressive capital deployment into assets.
